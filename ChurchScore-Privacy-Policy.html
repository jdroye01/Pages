Section 01

Overview & Scope

This Privacy Policy describes how ChurchScore ("the App," "we," "our") handles information when you use the ChurchScore iOS application. ChurchScore is a locally-hosted points and attendance tracking tool designed for church organizations.

This policy applies to all users of the ChurchScore iOS application. By using the App, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this policy.

✅
Self-hosted by design. ChurchScore is architected so that all data lives on infrastructure you control — your iPads and your own server. We, the developers, have no servers, no cloud accounts, and no systems through which your data passes.
Section 02

Information the App Stores

ChurchScore stores the following data locally on your device and, if configured, on your self-hosted server:

Data Type What It Includes Where It's Stored
Member Records Names, class assignments, profile photos Local SQLite on device; your server
Point Records Points awarded, dates, associated actions Local SQLite on device; your server
Class & Action Definitions Class names, action names, point values Local SQLite on device; your server
Tier Definitions Tier names and point thresholds Local SQLite on device; your server
Profile Photos Optional member photos Local device Documents folder only
Server URL The ws:// address of your sync server iOS UserDefaults (on device only)
Offline Queue Pending sync events when offline iOS UserDefaults (on device only)

All of the above data is entered by you and stored exclusively on your own hardware. None of it is transmitted to or accessible by the App's developers.

Section 03

Information We Do Not Collect

ChurchScore does not collect any of the following:

  • Usage analytics or behavioral data
  • Crash reports or diagnostics sent to the developer
  • Device identifiers (IDFA, IDFV, or similar)
  • Location data
  • Contact list or address book access
  • Microphone or camera data beyond photos explicitly selected by the user
  • IP addresses or network information
  • Any form of telemetry or performance monitoring
✅
No analytics SDKs. ChurchScore contains no third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking libraries. The only external network communication the app makes is to the server address you configure yourself.
Section 04

How Data Is Used

Data stored by ChurchScore is used solely to provide the features of the application:

  • Displaying member rosters and point totals
  • Calculating attendance statistics and tier status
  • Generating attendance and points reports
  • Synchronizing data in real time across multiple iPads on your network

No data is used for advertising, profiling, marketing, or any purpose beyond operating the App's stated features.

Section 05

Data Sharing & Third Parties

We do not share any data with third parties. ChurchScore has no relationship with advertising networks, data brokers, analytics providers, or any external services.

ℹ️
Your server, your rules. If you configure the optional real-time sync feature, data flows between your iPads and a server that you own and operate. The developers of ChurchScore have no access to, visibility into, or control over that server or the data it stores.

The only scenario in which data leaves the app is when you use the built-in export feature (PDF or CSV reports), which saves a file to your device or shares it via the standard iOS share sheet. You are solely responsible for how you handle exported files.

Section 06

Children's Privacy

ChurchScore is designed for use by church administrators and teachers. The App may store the names of minors (children who are church members) as part of its member roster functionality.

🛡️
COPPA Compliance. ChurchScore does not collect any information directly from children. The App is operated by adult church administrators. Member names entered into the system are managed entirely by those administrators on privately controlled infrastructure. No member data — including data relating to minors — is transmitted to the App's developers or any third party.

If you believe that information about a child has been entered into the system inappropriately, please contact your organization's ChurchScore administrator to have it removed from your local database and server.

Section 07

Data Security

Because ChurchScore stores data locally on your devices and on your own server, the security of that data is primarily your responsibility. We recommend the following best practices:

  • Keep your iPads updated to the latest version of iOS
  • Use a strong passcode or Face ID / Touch ID on each device
  • Run your sync server on a private network (e.g., Tailscale) rather than exposing it to the public internet
  • Keep your server operating system and Node.js installation up to date
  • Use a strong PostgreSQL database password
  • Regularly back up your server database

The App itself does not implement encryption of the local SQLite database beyond what iOS provides through its standard data protection APIs. Profile photos are stored unencrypted in the app's Documents directory.

Section 08

Data Retention & Deletion

Data is retained for as long as it exists on your device or your server. The App provides no automatic expiration of records.

To delete data:

  • Individual records — Use the delete functions within the App (swipe to delete in the Settings tab)
  • All local data — Delete the App from your iPad; iOS will remove the App's Documents directory and SQLite database
  • Server data — Connect to your PostgreSQL server and drop the database, or use your server's uninstall script

Because the developers have no access to your data, we cannot delete data on your behalf.

Section 09

Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding personal data:

Right How to Exercise It
Access All data is viewable directly within the App or by querying your local database
Correction Edit member names and records directly in the App's Settings tab
Deletion Delete individual records in the App, or uninstall the App to remove all local data
Portability Export records as CSV or PDF using the Reports tab
Objection Contact your organization's ChurchScore administrator

Because all data is stored on your own infrastructure, you have full and direct control over it at all times.

Section 10

Photo Library Access

ChurchScore may request access to your device's photo library or camera solely for the purpose of setting a profile photo for a church member. This access is:

  • Requested only when you tap a member's profile photo to change it
  • Used only to select or capture a single image
  • Not used to scan, index, upload, or analyze your photo library in any way

Selected photos are saved to the App's local Documents directory on your device. They are not transmitted to any server or external service. You can revoke camera and photo library access at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Photos / Camera.

Section 11

Network Communication

ChurchScore communicates over the network only in the following circumstances:

  • When connecting to the sync server address you configure in Settings. This is a server you own and operate.
  • When pulling a full data sync from your server via the REST endpoint on your server.
🌐
No external connections. ChurchScore makes no connections to any servers operated by the App's developers, Apple (beyond standard App Store functions), or any third party. All network traffic goes exclusively to the server address you provide.
